At first, because they were timid, our ancestors called thunder ‘Dad’, and rain ‘Mom’, creating the primitive worship of ‘animism’.

Later—

  • The ancient Egyptians deified their pets (jackals, cats).
  • The ancient Greeks, on the other hand, raised a group of ‘superpowered giant babies’ on Mount Olympus.

Zeus and his family, doing no real work, were busy every day with affairs and feuds.

But in the end, a more efficient and monopolistic business model emerged:

Monotheism

This logic is extremely self-consistent— there is only one ‘Big Boss’ in the heavens, and the mortal world only needs one ‘CEO’.

And the founder of this monopolistic enterprise was—Abraham (also known as Ibrahim).

I. A Family Feud: A Soap Opera That Changed Geopolitics#

All major events that changed the world might have initially started simply because—

The first wife couldn’t have children.

Abraham was a successful entrepreneur, but he had a secret sorrow: his first wife, Sarah, was infertile.

And so—

  • He had his firstborn son with the handmaiden Hagar: Ishmael.
  • Later, his first wife miraculously became pregnant and gave birth to a second son: Isaac.

To avoid a family inheritance dispute, Hagar and her son were exiled to the desert.

This ‘dispute between the legitimate and illegitimate heirs’ became the most expensive family separation in human history:

  • Isaac (the legitimate second son) → became the ancestor of the Jewish people → and incidentally produced Moses and Jesus among his descendants.
  • Ishmael (the illegitimate firstborn son) → became the ancestor of the Arab people → and from his descendants emerged the Prophet Muhammad.

A miraculous scientific coincidence: modern genetics (Y-chromosome haplogroups) has surprisingly corroborated this legend—

  • Jews are predominantly J2.
  • Arabs are predominantly J1.

These two genetic groups indeed shared the same ‘father’ 4000 years ago.

So it turns out, the earth-shattering conflict in the Middle East today is, in essence, just two brothers beating each other up over the inheritance rights to the ancestral home.

III. The Art of Interpretation: How Did Polygamy ‘Disappear’?#

Since the scripts are more or less the same, why are their ways of life worlds apart?

The answer is just one sentence:

The text is dead, but the right to interpret it is alive.

Take ‘polygamy’ as an example.

Many people think this is a ‘perk’ of a certain religion, but its originator is actually Judaism.

In Exodus 21:10, God even thoughtfully laid out a ‘Guide to Taking Concubines’:

“If he takes another wife to himself, he shall not diminish her food, her clothing, or her marital rights.”

King Solomon married 700 wives, and God didn’t expel him.

So why do Christians practice monogamy now?

  • Christianity’s ‘will to survive’ In order to proselytize in the Roman Empire, which practiced monogamy, it directly used its power of interpretation to forcibly change the rules.

The scriptures didn’t change; the CEO, in order to adapt to the market, made a breach-of-contract interpretation of a contract from thousands of years ago.
