    Religion: A 4,000-Year “War for the Right to Interpret”#

    In the early days of human civilization, religion was like a chaotic ‘startup competition’.

    Initially, our ancestors were timid:

    • They called the one who thunders ‘father’
    • They called the one who rains ‘mother’

    Thus, they created the primitive worship of ‘animism’.

    Later—

    • The ancient Egyptians deified their pets (jackals, cats)
    • The ancient Greeks raised a group of ‘superpowered man-children’ on Mount Olympus

    Zeus and his family, aside from doing any real work, were busy cheating and bickering all day.

    But eventually, a more efficient and monopolistic business model emerged:

    Monotheism

    This logic was extremely self-consistent— There is only one ‘Big Boss’ in heaven, and the mortal world only needs one ‘CEO’.

    And the founder of this monopolistic enterprise was— Abraham (also known as Ibrahim)

    I. A Family Feud: The Soap Opera That Changed Geopolitics#

    All major world-changing events might have started simply because—

    The first wife couldn’t have children.

    Abraham was a successful entrepreneur, but his first wife, Sarah, was infertile. So:

    • He had his first son with the handmaiden Hagar: Ishmael
    • Later, his first wife miraculously became pregnant and gave birth to a second son: Isaac

    To avoid a family inheritance dispute, Hagar and her son were exiled to the desert.

    This ‘dispute between the legitimate and illegitimate heirs’ became the most expensive family separation in human history:

    • Isaac (the legitimate second son) → Became the ancestor of the Jewish people → And incidentally produced Moses and Jesus among his descendants

    • Ishmael (the illegitimate first son) → Became the ancestor of the Arab people → And from his descendants emerged the Prophet Muhammad

    A Surprising Scientific Coincidence#

    Modern genetics (Y-chromosome haplogroups) has surprisingly corroborated this legend:

    • Jewish people are predominantly J2
    • Arab people are predominantly J1

    These two genetic groups did indeed share the same ‘father’ 4,000 years ago.

    So, the earth-shattering conflict in the Middle East today is, in essence, just two brothers fighting over the family inheritance.

    III. The Art of Interpretation: How Did Polygamy “Disappear”?#

    The text is dead, but the right to interpret it is alive.

    Take ‘polygamy’ for example.

    Many people think this is a ‘perk’ of a certain religion, but its originator was actually Judaism. In Exodus 21:10, God even thoughtfully provided a ‘guide to taking concubines’:

    “If he takes another wife to himself, he shall not diminish her food, her clothing, or her marital rights.”

    King Solomon married 700 wives, and God didn’t fire him.

    So why do Christians practice monogamy now?

    • Christianity’s ‘survival instinct’ → To spread the faith in the Roman Empire, which practiced monogamy, they simply used their right of interpretation to forcibly change the rules

    The scripture didn’t change. The ‘CEO,’ in order to adapt to the market, made a breach-of-contract interpretation of a thousands-of-years-old agreement.
