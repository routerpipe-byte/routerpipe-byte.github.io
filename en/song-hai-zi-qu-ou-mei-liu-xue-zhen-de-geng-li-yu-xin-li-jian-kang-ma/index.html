Even accounting for differences in statistical scope, the US rate is still significantly higher.\n\n \n\n—\n\n## 04 The AI Admits Its "Mistake"\n\nWhen I presented this data to the AI, it frankly admitted:\n\n> "My previous conclusion was based more on assumptions from my training data and general public opinion—that ‘high stress = poor mental health’—rather than reasoning from hard data."\n\nIt also stated that in the future, it would base its reasoning on objective data to avoid preconceived judgments and be truly scientific, objective, and fair.\n\nThis conversation left a deep impression on me: many parents, teachers, and even experts often rely on intuition rather than evidence when discussing "mental health." In contrast, an overly relaxed environment may not help children deal with real-world pressures, potentially making them more fragile when they encounter genuine difficulties.\n\n—\n\n## 07 Takeaways for Parents\n\n* Don’t panic about pressure: Moderate academic stress can cultivate a sense of purpose and resilience.\n* Mental health isn’t just about "having fun": A regular routine, engagement in learning, and family support are more crucial.\n* Studying abroad is not a panacea: Adolescent suicide rates are higher in the West.
